Reflections on travelling through Iceland

When my sister Nancy and I were younger we spent a lot of time outside camping, hiking, sailing and canoeing. I loved moving all day, having space to think, and it’s a part of my life that I’ve missed a lot since moving to Toronto.


For the past two years Nancy and I have been living in different cities and since then we haven’t had the chance to hang out for long stretches of time. At the end of May we decided to meet up and head to Iceland. Within a few days we had to find a place to stay, and as for the rest we decided to wing it.

Technology

Though we went for the landscape I found myself blown away by the elegant uses of technology. It’s all so seamless. The renewable energy fuels the country and everything is planned around it, but technology also seeps into the historic and cultural sites. We went to go see ruins of early Icelandic settlements where augmented reality was used to show the structure of what the buildings originally looked like. We entered another 365 degree room to experience the northern lights and so many other wilderness scenes. It wasn’t just technology for technology’s sake, we were really delighted by the experiences.
